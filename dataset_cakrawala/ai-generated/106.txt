Chicago police were questioning two people Tuesday morning in connection with Monday night's attack on a Chicago Transit Authority train.

Two men were being questioned in the attack, which occurred about 11:30 p.m. Monday on a Blue Line train bound for Forest Park.

Roderick Wilson, 18, of the 100 block of North May Street was charged with armed robbery, aggravated battery and unlawful use of a weapon. Wilson was arrested about 2 a.m. Tuesday.

Marquise Marshall, 21, of the 1600 block of South Francisco Avenue was charged with armed robbery and unlawful use of a weapon. Marshall was arrested about 2:30 a.m. Tuesday.

Investigators said they believe the men knew the victim.

No one was injured in the attack.

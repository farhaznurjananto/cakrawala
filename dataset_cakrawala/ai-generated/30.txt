How does The Silmarillion as published differ from the Silmarillion J.R.R. Tolkien intended to publish?

The Silmarillion as published differs from the Silmarillion J.R.R. Tolkien intended to publish in a number of ways. Most notably, it includes a number of editorial changes made by Christopher Tolkien in order to make the work more accessible to a wider audience. In addition, the published version also includes a number of previously unpublished works, such as the "Ainulindalë" and the "Akallabêth". Finally, the published version includes a number of appendices and footnotes, which were not included in the original manuscript. Overall, these changes render the published version a significantly different work from the original manuscript.

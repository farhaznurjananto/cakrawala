Hyundai and Kia are finally making cars that car fans will be interested in. The Hyundai Elantra GT Sport is the newest car like this. It just made its debut today at the Chicago Auto Show. It's not the hard-core hot hatch we've been waiting for, but it should do well against the Mazda3, Honda Civic Sport, and Volkswagen Golf. Advertisement - Read More Below Hyundai The Elantra GT Sport has the same 1.6-liter turbo four-cylinder engine as the Elantra Sport sedan that came out last year. This engine makes 201 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque. The GT Sport comes with both a six-speed manual and a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission. The base Elantra GT hatch has a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine with 162 hp and either a six-speed manual or a six-speed torque-converter automatic transmission. Hyundai Hyundai says that both the base GT and the GT Sport were made at the Nurburgring, which gives them a great balance between how they ride and how they handle. Also, the rear suspension of the base car has torsion bars, but the GT Sport has an independent multilink setup. Hyundai Scott Margason, head of U.S. products for Hyundai, called the Elantra GT Sport a "true hot hatch" in a press release, but we won't know for sure until we drive one. Based on the specs, the Elantra GT Sport doesn't seem to be as good as the Volkswagen Golf GTI, which has 220 horsepower, or the Ford Focus ST, which has 252 horsepower. But even against the base Golf, Honda Civic Sport, and Mazda3 S, it faces some tough competition from other hatchbacks.

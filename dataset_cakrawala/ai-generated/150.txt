The tenth Hackers on Planet Earth conference will take place in New York City, at Hotel Pennsylvania, on July 18 through July 20, 2008. 
Hackers on Planet Earth (HOPE) is a hackers' conference. The kinds of hackers who participate are programmers, networking and communications specialists, electronics engineers, artists, makers, and builders of all kinds. The conference is organized by members of the global hacker community who wish to bring the challenges and opportunities presented by technology to the public eye. 
The conference is an opportunity for hackers to share their knowledge and ideas with the public through lectures and workshops, as well as to network with fellow hackers from around the world. The conference is also a chance for the public to learn about hacking and to meet the people who are passionate about technology and its potential. 
HOPE is organized by the hacker collective 2600. For more information about the conference, please visit the HOPE website: http://www.2600.com/hope/ or contact us at info@2600.com. 
The conference will include the following tracks:

- Technology
- Security
- Art and Culture
- DIY
- Hackers and the Media
- Hacking for Social Change
- Hackerspaces and Open Hardware
- The Future of Hackerspace Technology
- and more! 
Registration for the conference is open now at: http://www.2600.com/hope/register.html. The cost of the conference is $250 for members of the global hacker community and $350 for non-members. Early bird rates are available until May 31. After that, the regular price is $350 for members and $450 for non-members. Please visit the website for more information. 
We hope to see you at the tenth Hackers on Planet Earth conference! 
Sincerely,
The Hackers on Planet Earth team.
